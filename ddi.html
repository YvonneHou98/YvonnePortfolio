Project Overview

DDI team partnered with Dexcom, a company that develops, manufactures, and distributes continuous glucose monitoring systems for diabetes management. We aim to empowered diabetes patients community through ideate, interact with users, iterate, and improve, generating a clickable prototype at the end of the 7 one-week sprints.

All of our prototypes and design solutions are protected under NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) and Dexcom needs one year to ship. Learn more on this official website released by Dexcom about our work & process.

My role

My role in DDI is undergrad UX researcher and humble learner from the Dexcom users in the area of diabetes.


My Takeaways

• Designing for users in a field you don't already know is NOT intimidating. ( I once thought it is ) Unfamiliarity is actually a good thing, where you don't have any pre-existing assumptions to biase your design, and you have to approach users with complete UX research process: listen, empathize, and solve their problem with considerate design solutions.

• This experience triggers my interest in healthcare technology and how considerate human-centered design approach can facilitate user experience. Building technology with the warmth of humanity is fanscinating to me.

• I appreciate the resource Dexcom brought to us, allowing us to interact and test our prototypes on real-world users: type-1 diabetes recruited by Dexcom. Helped us give solution to reduce the technical support phone call volumn and improve the user troubleshooting experience.
